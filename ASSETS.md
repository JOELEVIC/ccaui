# CCA Asset Guide

Use **royalty-free / CC0 / CC-BY** sources only (e.g. Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay), or CCA-owned/licensed assets. Do not use unlicensed images of identifiable people.

## Image map

| File / slot | Role | Suggested content | Where to get |
|-------------|------|-------------------|--------------|
| `images/hero.jpg` | Hero background or right panel | Chess board in a Cameroonian setting, or Mount Cameroon / Douala skyline with dark overlay | Unsplash/Pexels: "chess board", "chess Africa", "Cameroon landscape", "Douala", "Yaounde" |
| `images/play-compete.jpg` | Play & compete section left panel | In-progress game or tournament hall, or Cameroon university building | Stock: "chess tournament", "university Cameroon" |
| `images/rankings-bg.jpg` (optional) | National rankings preview | Subtle texture or players at a board (generic) | Placeholder or one hero image; avoid identifiable students unless CCA provides |
| `images/academy-*.jpg` (optional) | The Academy pillars | Governance, tournaments, development (e.g. building, trophy, youth) | Abstract or stock; or small illustrations |
| `images/cameroon-chess.jpg` (optional) | "Cameroon & chess" section | Chess in Cameroon (youth, school, federation event) | CCA-provided or stock "chess school Africa" |
| Footer crest | Identity | Cameroon crest or flag motif (simplified, gold + green/red accent) | Designed asset or simple SVG from public domain flag description |

## Video (optional)

| File | Role | Suggested content |
|------|------|-------------------|
| `videos/hero-loop.mp4` | Hero or About background | Short loop: abstract (pieces moving) or B-roll of chess play; muted, autoplay, low opacity. 10–20 s. |

**Sourcing:** Pexels/Pixabay video, or CCA-produced. Store in `public/videos/`.

## World chess superstars section

Use only **official FIDE/player sites**, **Wikipedia (check licence)**, or **purchased/credited stock**. Prefer placeholder frames and replace with licensed images and approved quotes. Suggested: Magnus Carlsen, Judit Polgár, Vishy Anand, or African/Grand Masters.
